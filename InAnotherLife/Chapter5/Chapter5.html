Basics of war
- MV Wilhelm Gustloff was a German military transport ship, sunk on 30 January 1945 by Soviet submarine S-13 in the Baltic Sea while evacuating civilians and military personnel.
- ORP Orzeł was an Orzeł class submarine of the Polish Navy that served during World War II. Orzeł entered, in September 1939, the harbour of neutral Estonia. The Estonian authorities tried to intern the submarine, under pressure from Germany and the Soviet Union, but it escaped.
- The Miracle on the Vistula: 1920 war between Poland and Soviets.
- Stalin's purge of 1930s.
- Operation Barabarossa: Hitler attacks the Soviets.
- Soviet invasion of Poland: 16 days after Hitler invaded Poland from the west, Stalin invaded Poland from the east. They carved out Poland into two parts as their own spheres of influence. It stayed this way until Hitler invaded the soviets under the Operation Barbarossa.
- Hitler's rescue of Mussolini.
- Lend-Lease Act - The Lend-Lease Act was signed into law on March 11, 1941, and ended on September 20, 1945. A total of $50.1 billion (equivalent to $690 billion in 2024 when accounting for inflation) worth of supplies was shipped, or 17% of the total war expenditures of the U.S. In all, $31.4 billion went to the United Kingdom, $11.3 billion to the Soviet Union, $3.2 billion to France, $1.6 billion to China, and the remaining $2.6 billion to other Allies. Reverse Lend-Lease was the supply of equipment and services to the United States. Nearly $8 billion (equivalent to $124 billion today) worth of war material was provided to U.S. forces by its allies, 90% of this sum coming from the British Commonwealth Empire - mostly India and Australia.
